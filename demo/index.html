Kairu

Make AI responses 2× faster by generating multiple words at once.

Kairu is a small, open-source library that speeds up large language models. It guesses ahead, verifies in parallel, and stops thinking the moment it is confident — so your users wait less.

how it works

Three steps. No magic.

The trick is splitting the work between two models — a small fast one that guesses and a large slow one that checks the guesses in one shot.

1

A fast guesser predicts ahead

A small AI proposes the next 4 words it thinks will come — it is quick but not always right.

2

The main model checks all 4 at once

Instead of generating one word at a time, the main model looks at the 4 guesses together — that is the speed-up.

3

Keep the good guesses, redo the rest

Whatever the main model agrees with becomes part of the answer. Wrong guesses are tossed and re-rolled.

second trick

Stop thinking when the answer is obvious

A modern AI processes every word through dozens of layers. But for easy words — like finishing the phrase “New York City” after “New York” — the model is sure after just a few layers. Kairu lets it stop early.

How to read this

Each rising column is one word being generated. It climbs through the layers of the model. The moment its confidence crosses the green line, it lights up “done!” and stops — saving every layer above.

what is in the box

Six composable building blocks

Use one. Use all six. Each one ships with tests, stats, and a documented API.

⚡

Draft + verify

Generate a guess with a small model, check it instantly with a big one. Most guesses are right — that is your speedup.

kairu.SpeculativeDecoder

⏩

Stop when confident

Halt halfway through the model when the answer is already obvious. Easy words skip up to 60% of the work.

kairu.LayerwiseEarlyExitDecoder

💾

Remember answers

Cache what the model already computed for a given prefix. The next request that starts the same way is instant.

kairu.LogitsCache · CachedModel

🎯

Auto-tune lookahead

If the guesser is on a streak, guess further. If it is missing, pull back. Same idea as TCP congestion control.

kairu.DynamicGammaScheduler

🧭

Pick the right strategy

Tell Kairu about your model and it picks the best mix of techniques — with a one-line explanation of why.

kairu.AutoProfile

🌊

Stream to the browser

A built-in server speaks the same protocol as OpenAI — drop it in and your existing client code keeps working.

kairu.create_app · POST /generate
